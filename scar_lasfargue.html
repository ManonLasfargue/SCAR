About SCAR

SCAR (Scenarios for Carbon Assessment in Research) is an academic platform created to help research teams quantify and visualize the carbon footprint of their projects. It was developed by Manon Lasfargue at Université PSL as part of a research initiative on sustainable scientific practices. The platform uses verified emission factors from institutions such as ADEME and GES 1point5 to ensure scientific reliability and reproducibility in its calculations.

Objectives

The goal of SCAR is to promote climate-conscious decision-making within the research community. By identifying key emission sources such as mobility, digital activities, and energy consumption, SCAR allows users to evaluate their environmental impact and explore alternative low-carbon strategies. The project aims to support more sustainable research policies and encourage transparent reporting within academic institutions.

Methodology

SCAR’s methodology is based on a modular calculation model integrating multiple categories of emissions. Through an intuitive online form, users provide information related to travel, IT infrastructure, energy use, and food. The system computes emissions using standardized coefficients and produces visual representations for analysis and comparison. The tool is designed to be scalable, allowing future updates as emission factors evolve or new data becomes available.
